This Is Not A War, It's A Genocide

The ongoing tragedy in Palestine, particularly in Gaza, is not a conventional war between equal forces but a systematic, calculated destruction of an entire people and culture. For decades, Palestinians have faced forced displacement, restricted access to basic necessities, and unrelenting violence under military occupation. Entire communities are being eradicated, homes are reduced to rubble, and families live with the constant fear of loss, while essential infrastructure like schools, hospitals, and power facilities are systematically destroyed. With borders sealed and aid severely limited, civilians are trapped in conditions that leave no possibility for escape or survival. This cycle of destruction—targeting generations of Palestinian people, culture, and heritage—is a clear violation of human rights. The reality is undeniable: what is happening in Palestine is not a war between two forces but an unfolding genocide that demands immediate and resolute global action to stop the systematic erasure of an entire population.

It Did Not Start On Ontober 7.

The Nakba ("Catastrophe") - 1948

In 1948, the creation of the State of Israel led to the forced displacement of approximately 750,000 Palestinians from their homes. Hundreds of Palestinian villages were destroyed, erasing cultural and historical heritage. This mass displacement laid the foundation for the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. - Read more

Occupation of the West Bank and Gaza Strip - 1967

The Six-Day War resulted in Israel occupying the West Bank, Gaza Strip, East Jerusalem, and the Golan Heights. Palestinians living in these areas came under military rule, which subjected them to severe restrictions on movement, land confiscation, and lack of legal protection. - Read more

Expansion of Israeli Settlements - 1970s-Present

Since the 1970s, Israel has built numerous settlements in the occupied territories. These settlements are illegal under international law, and they fragment Palestinian communities, making everyday life difficult and eroding the possibility of establishing a contiguous Palestinian state. - Read more

First Intifada (Uprising) - 1987-1993

The First Intifada, a Palestinian uprising against Israeli occupation, highlighted the frustrations of Palestinians living under occupation. Israeli forces responded with significant force, which led to thousands of casualties, a crackdown on Palestinian civil society, and further restriction of rights. - Read more

Oslo Accords - 1993-1995

The Oslo Accords aimed to establish peace but instead formalized control over Palestinian lands, with the West Bank divided into Areas A, B, and C. Area C, which covers more than 60% of the West Bank, remained under full Israeli control, limiting Palestinian development and access to land. - Read more

Second Intifada - 2000-2005

A new uprising erupted due to the failure of peace negotiations, resulting in widespread violence. Israel intensified its military response, leading to thousands of Palestinian deaths, further destruction of infrastructure, and the construction of a separation wall that segregates and restricts Palestinian movement. - Read more

Blockade of Gaza - 2007-Present

Following Hamas' election victory in Gaza, Israel and Egypt imposed a severe blockade on the Gaza Strip. This blockade restricts the movement of people and goods, creating a humanitarian crisis marked by shortages of food, medicine, and essential services. - Read more

Bombardments of Gaza

The bombing of the Gaza Strip is an ongoing aerial bombardment campaign on the Gaza Strip by the Israeli Air Force during the Israel-Hamas war. During the bombing, Israeli airstrikes damaged or destroyed Palestinian refugee camps, schools, hospitals, mosques, churches, and other civilian infrastructure. By late April 2024 it was estimated that Israel had dropped over 70,000 tons of bombs over Gaza, surpassing the bombing of Dresden, Hamburg, and London combined during World War II - Read more

Settler Violence and Daily Discrimination - Ongoing

Palestinians in the West Bank face ongoing harassment, violence from settlers, and discrimination. Many live under constant threat of home demolitions, forced evictions, and land seizures. Military checkpoints and permit systems fragment the West Bank and severely limit Palestinians' freedom of movement. - Read more

Forced Evictions in East Jerusalem - 2021-Present

In neighborhoods like Sheikh Jarrah, Palestinian families face eviction to make way for Israeli settlers, sparking international condemnation. These evictions are part of a broader policy to assert control over East Jerusalem and reduce the Palestinian presence in the city. - Read more

Current Crisis in Gaza - October 2023

The violence and civilian casualties resulting from the October 2023 crisis in Gaza are part of a longstanding pattern of suffering and destruction. The escalation reflects unresolved tensions that stem from decades of occupation, displacement, and systemic inequities faced by Palestinians. - Read more
